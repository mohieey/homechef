The funky depth of blue cheese and the sweet tang of caramelized onion, then doused in red wine, is a marriage made in heaven. They bring their love to the meaty burger, and all three worm their way into your heart once you have one bite. This meal is really a love connection all the way through.

In Your Box (serves 2)

  • 2 Russet Potatoes
  • 1 Red Onion
  • 2 oz. Red Cooking Wine
  • ½ oz. Baby Arugula
  • 1 Rosemary Sprig
  • 10 oz. Ground Beef
  • 2 Brioche Buns
  • 1 oz. Blue Cheese Crumbles
  • 1 tsp. Sugar
  • ½ tsp. Garlic Pepper

  • Seafood 145° F |
  • Chicken 165° F |
  • Ground Beef 160° F |

Customize It Instructions

If using 20 oz. ground beef, follow same instructions as 10 oz. ground beef, forming 4 patties and stacking 2 to serve.

If using ground turkey, follow same instructions as ground beef in Steps 2 and 5, cooking until turkey reaches minimum internal temperature, 6-8 minutes per side.

If using ground pork, follow same instructions as ground beef in Steps 2 and 5, cooking until pork reaches minimum internal temperature, 4-6 minutes per side.

If using Impossible burger, follow same instructions as ground beef in Steps 2 and 5, cooking until burger is heated through, 4-6 minutes per side.

Roast the Fries

Cut potatoes into ¼" fries and pat dry.

Place fries on prepared baking sheet and toss with 1 Tbsp. olive oil, garlic pepper, 1/4 tsp. salt, and a pinch of pepper. Massage oil and seasoning into fries.

Spread into a single layer. Roast in hot oven until lightly browned, 25-28 minutes, tossing fries halfway through.

Carefully remove fries from oven and toss with 1 tsp. minced rosemary (prepared in a later step). Baking sheet will be hot! Use a utensil.

While potatoes roast, continue recipe.

Prepare the Ingredients

Stem and mince rosemary.

Halve and peel onion. Slice halves into thin strips.

Form ground beef into two patties, about 5" in diameter. Season both sides with a pinch of salt and pepper.

Cook the Onion

Place a medium non-stick pan over medium heat and add 2 tsp. olive oil.

Add onion and a pinch of salt to hot pan. Stir occasionally until softened and browned, 10-12 minutes.

Add sugar and wine. Bring to a simmer.

Once simmering, stir occasionally until wine is almost entirely evaporated, 1-2 minutes.

If pan becomes dry, add water, 1 Tbsp. at a time, as needed.

Remove from burner.

While onions cook, continue recipe.

Toast the Buns

Place a large non-stick pan over medium heat and add 1 tsp. olive oil.

Add buns, cut side down, to hot pan. Toast until lightly browned, 1-2 minutes.

Transfer buns to a plate. Keep pan over medium heat.

Cook Patties and Finish Dish

Add 1 tsp. olive oil and patties to hot pan. Cook until browned and patties reach a minimum internal temperature of 160 degrees, 4-6 minutes per side.

Remove from burner. Top patties evenly with cheese (to taste). Cover until melted, 1-2 minutes.

Plate dish as pictured on front of card, topping bottom bun with onion, patty, arugula, and top bun. Bon appétit!
